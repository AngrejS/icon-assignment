Here's How You Can Help Afghan Refugees

Volunteer to help resettled refugees: If you are able to provide temporary or permanent housing or would like to support Afghan families in your community in any other way please fill out this form by HIAS. Airbnb has been providing temporary housing to Afghan refugees and displaced people. If you’re interested in this program, click here to learn more. You can offer to host a place, or “donate to help cover the cost of temporary accommodations.”

Email the White House: The International Rescue Committee has an email form through which you can urge the Biden administration to take immediate steps to ensure "vulnerable Afghans have pathways to safety,” including those who risked their lives to help the U.S.

Consider donating: The International Rescue Committee, are helping families in Afghanistan and worldwide, and are transparent about how each dollar is spent; Women for Afghan Women, who are the largest women’s organization in Afghanistan and they provide resources to those affected in the United States; Afghan Diaspora for Equality and Progress, which was formed right after the 2016 election of President Trump, are accepting donations to help cover the costs of application fees (Application fee for each person is $575 – which means full family applications can become more expensive.) You can also donate items such as kitchenware, food, cleaning products, laptops and school supplies, furniture, household goods, toys and items for kids for those resettling in your community. Among other ways you can provide is through language assistance, job training and housing assistance.

Call your local elected officials: Pick up the phone and dial (844) 236-2373. This number will direct you straight to their offices. Introduce where you are from, ask for additional governmental support such as funds, better housing, accessibility to healthcare and resources, a clear path to greencards or citizenships, and more. Urge immediate action to help expand Afghan resettlement programs and distribute resources equitably. Ask your family and friends to join in calling their elected officials. The more phone calls, the better.

Sign petitions: Send a message to members of congress to cosponsor the Welcomed Act. This act will help ensure that Afghans do not suffer as a result of the rushed evacuation. If passed by Congress and signed into law by the president, the bill will provide evacuated Afghans with the same basic services and resources other refugees and Special Immigrant Visa recipients have traditionally received and that Congress has mandated for them, according to Lutheran Immigration and Refugee Service.
